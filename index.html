A six-part series

The Staff of Ten

By day I help lead video operations. At home, I run ten AI agents — a rental business, a leashed paper-trading bot, a house counsel, a bartender, and one agent that belongs to my wife. This is how it works, and why you should build your own.

Alan Munro · July 2026

Start here. Nobody was going to hand me an AI education, so I built my own curriculum: a staff of specialized agents doing real work — real bookings, simulated trading guardrails, real inventories — with me as the manager. None of them took a job from anyone; almost all of them do work that was never practical for a human to do at all. If your not already building AI projects for work, skip "hello world" and build something useful for yourself. The skills come along for free.

For the technically curious: the chassis is OpenClaw, a self-hosted agent framework on a Mac mini; the everyday brains come through OpenRouter.ai's model marketplace; and the two desks that handle the most sensitive categories use direct, named-model connections to Anthropic and OpenAI. Part 1 shows the stack, part 5 explains the split.
